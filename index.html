The Problem

A City That Wastes Food While People Go Hungry

Every day, tons of perfectly good food are thrown away by restaurants and grocery stores — while thousands of families skip meals. The disconnect isn't about scarcity. It's about logistics.

35%

Food Wasted

Over a third of all food produced in the U.S. is never eaten — tossed at restaurants, warehouses, and grocery shelves while still perfectly edible.

1 in 8

Families Struggle

One in eight households in our region faces food insecurity — parents skipping meals so their children can eat, seniors choosing between food and medication.

4 hrs

The Freshness Window

Surplus food has a narrow rescue window. Without a fast, coordinated pickup system, edible meals become landfill waste within hours of closing time.

Our Story

From Campus Idea to Community Lifeline

What started as a computer science project to reduce campus dining hall waste became a city-wide food rescue network powered by technology and heart.

1

The Campus Spark

It began when our founder noticed hundreds of untouched meals discarded nightly from the university cafeteria — and built a prototype app to connect that surplus with a nearby shelter.

2

The First Rescue Run

Within two weeks, 15 student volunteers were picking up surplus food from three campus dining halls and delivering 200+ meals weekly to two local shelters.

3

Going City-Wide

Restaurants and grocery stores noticed. Within six months, the network expanded to 50+ food donors across the city, and the mobile app matched pickups in real time.

4

TableSpark Is Born

The project incorporated as TableSpark — a nonprofit scaling its technology to serve every neighborhood, partnering with farms for fresh produce, and running weekly community meal drives.

Programs

How We Turn Surplus Into Sustenance

Four interconnected programs that form a complete food rescue ecosystem — from alert to doorstep.

Real-Time Rescue

SparkRoute

Our smart-routing engine sends instant alerts when restaurants, grocery stores, or caterers have surplus food. Volunteers claim pickups through the app and deliver within the 4-hour freshness window using optimized routes.

Farm to Family

SeedBox

Every week, partner farms donate imperfect but nutritious produce that won't make retail shelves. We pack curated SeedBoxes and distribute them directly to low-income families at neighborhood hubs.

Community Kitchens

RiseKitchen

Pop-up cooking events where volunteer chefs transform rescued ingredients into nutritious meals. Held weekly at community centers, these gatherings also teach participants how to cook affordable, low-waste meals at home.

Tech Platform

CrestConnect

The backbone of our operation — a mobile app that instantly matches food donors with available volunteer drivers. Real-time tracking, photo verification, and impact logging ensure every meal is accounted for.

About Us

Meet the Founder

test test

Founder & Executive Director · Computer Science

As a computer science student, test test saw a massive disconnect: restaurants throwing away hundreds of meals each night while shelters just blocks away struggled to feed people. The answer wasn't more food — it was better logistics.

That insight led to a prototype app connecting surplus food with volunteer drivers, which grew into TableSpark — a full-scale nonprofit using technology to make food rescue fast, reliable, and scalable.

"The food is already there. The people who need it are already there. We just had to build the bridge between them — and make it fast enough that nothing spoils along the way."
